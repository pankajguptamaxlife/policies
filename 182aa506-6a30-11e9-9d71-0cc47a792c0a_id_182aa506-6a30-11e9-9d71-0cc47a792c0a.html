Frequently Asked Questions:
Q. Why is there a minimum Flexible Benefits Plan decided to avail this benefit?
A. This is an outcome of discussions between the leasing company and Max Life and ensures that the monthly lease rentals are
easily supported from the employee Flexible Benefits Plan balance.
Q. Can I take any car model that I want?
A. Employee should be careful to ensure that the car model is in line not only with the employeeâ€™s choice but the corresponding
monthly lease rental can be deducted conveniently from Flexible Benefits Plan balance.
Q. Can I take car of higher value by paying the differential up front?
A. Yes, this is possible through appropriate adjustment to the upfront deposit amount to be paid to dealer. However, Tranzlease
(TZL) / Leasingiq (LIQ) would have the absolute discretion in this regard.
Q. How long will the process take after applying for the car lease?
A. Subject to the documentation being complete as per the process outlined in the policy, it would take around 5-8 days for the
process to be completed. Details of the exact delivery date would be intimated by Tranzlease (TZL) / Leasingiq (LIQ). Delivery of
car is subject to availability of the model with the dealer.
Q. If my Flexible Benefits Plan limit is not enough to fund the Lease Rental, Do I have the option of getting the differential
deducted from the monthly salary?
A. No, this is not permissible. The Lease Rental has to be funded through Flexible Benefits Plan and the differential cannot be
deducted through payroll.
Q. What happens if I leave before the lease period is over?
A. In such a scenario the employee will have to sign a fresh agreement with the vendor or need to foreclose the lease. Max Life
will be no longer a party to the same.
Q. In case I have to relocate / move then will the company bear any cost incurred in moving the car from current to my new
location?
A. Any and all costs incurred on account of movement - transportation, registration, any taxes, service tax on the monthly lease
rental will be debited to employees.
Q. Can I upgrade to a new car before the lease period is over?
A. Yes, but the employee would need to first foreclose the existing car under the car lease scheme.
